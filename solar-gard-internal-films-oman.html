It is absolutely hard to maintain the same temperature in the bigger office interiors unless you block the root cause of “Hot Spots” (The absorbed solar radiation is continuously emitted upto an extend of 2 meters from the office or home glass windows). No More window blinds & curtains

Most of the modern high rise buildings are constructed with glass walls or partitions for the better outlook with natural light and to enjoy the external scenery of aerial view. But, the exposure to the sun and solar heat may make the interior warmer and force the tenants to use preventive actions such as window curtains and blinds unknowingly.

Off course, the window curtain or blinds will block sun rays as well as your sun light and valuable external view. Many of us are still not aware that, a clear Solar Gard heat rejection window film can block the annoying solar heat & UV rays and keep your interior cooler. Solar Gard Saint-Gobain window films are with UV inhibitor properties to block the UVA & UVB rays which are harmful to the human body in excessive exposure.

Solar energy through a window is transferred or transmitted by infrared waves. Radiant energy is converted to heat when it strikes people or objects. Sun control window film blocks the transfer of this energy you keep it from turning into heat because it never strikes an object. Window film interrupts the transfer of this energy by reflecting the energy back. Solar window film installed on the inside of a window, the proportions are changed dramatically and improve your interior comfort.

Reduce the risk of glass breaks

In many parts of the world, natural disaster such as earthquake or hurricane is un predicted. Ground motion or the flying objects can cause the pressure on the glass and break apart to flying glass shards, endangering people below or rear most. Most of the injuries happens in such occurrence are due to flying broken glass shards than the real impact. Window films do not allow the glass to break apart and prevent the glass from flying in case of such impacts.
